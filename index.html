I don't just write specs — I build

I'm the PM who opens Figma, spins up a prototype, and walks into the engineering review with something tangible. My workflow: design → prototype → validate → spec → ship.

For internal or non-core tools, I build and ship directly with AI-assisted tooling. For core product features, I prototype first, validate with stakeholders, then hand engineering a tested spec. Zero wasted sprint cycles.

Prototype track
Design and validate before a line of code

For core features: prototype in Lovable, get stakeholder sign-off, write a PRD with acceptance criteria engineers can ship from. This catches wrong assumptions before they become wrong code.

What I believe

01

Revenue systems need guardrails, not just flexibility. Every edge case you don't design for becomes an incident.

02

Architecture decisions compound. Get them right early — or spend three quarters untangling them.

03

Build platforms, not one-off account hacks. If you're solving it once for one client, you're doing it wrong.

04

Use AI where it saves real time, not where it sounds cool. The prototype track is real. The hype isn't the point.
